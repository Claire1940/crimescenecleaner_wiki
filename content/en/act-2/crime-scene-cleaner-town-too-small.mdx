The "Town Too Small" mission marks a pivotal moment in the Act 2 update of **Crime Scene Cleaner**. After the intense events of previous chapters, players find themselves summoned to a local bowling alley for a job involving an old acquaintance: Big Jim. While the map is technically rated as a 1/4 in size, don't let the scale fool you. Completing the **crime scene cleaner town too small** level requires a meticulous eye for detail, as blood is splattered in high-reach areas and evidence is tucked away in clever hiding spots.

In this **crime scene cleaner town too small** walkthrough, we will cover everything from body disposal and evidence collection to locating the elusive secret rooms and collectible CDs. Whether you are aiming for a "Perfect" rating or just trying to survive the narrative twists of Act 2, this guide provides the professional strategies needed to scrub the alley clean.

## Mission Overview: Town Too Small

Before you unholster your pressure washer, it is vital to understand the scope of the job. This mission features a high body count relative to its small footprint, meaning you will be doing a lot of heavy lifting in a cramped environment.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Check the ceilings! This mission frequently places blood splatters on the roof and high walls, which can easily prevent you from hitting 100% cleanliness if overlooked.

## Step-by-Step Walkthrough

### 1. The Entrance and Corridor
Upon arriving, you'll notice the scene is already chaotic. Start by clearing the trash near the entrance to create a clear path for your bin. There is a significant amount of blood in the main corridor leading toward the kitchen and party room. 

### 2. The Kitchen and Secret Vent
The kitchen is one of the most important rooms for completionists. Before you start mopping, clear the trash piles. You will find a vent/grate on the wall that can be broken. Crawling through this vent leads to a hidden room containing a bed and a collectible CD. 

### 3. The Party Room and Tricky Furniture
The Party Room is a disaster zone of overturned chairs and broken tables. One of the biggest hurdles in the **crime scene cleaner town too small** level is finding all the furniture pieces. 
*   **The Chairs:** Most belong in the party room, but players often miss the one located outside or the one perched on top of the arcade machines.
*   **Blood Cleanup:** Use the "Dzonator" (your robotic cleaning friend) here to handle the floor while you focus on the walls and the undersides of the tables.

### 4. The Bowling Lanes and Maintenance Room
The bowling alley itself contains several bodies and a high concentration of evidence. Once the lanes are clear, head to the room behind the lanes. You will find a **door handle** sitting on a desk. This is a key item. Take this handle to the maintenance room (the one blocked by wooden boxes) and attach it to the door to unlock the final secret area.

## Finding the "Missing" Trash and Furniture

Many players find themselves stuck at 99% completion in "Town Too Small" due to small items. Here is a checklist of the most commonly missed objects:

*   **Shurikens and Darts:** These are incredibly thin and often blend into the carpet or get stuck in walls. Use your flashlight at a low angle to spot their shadows.
*   **The Arcade Chair:** Look on top of the video game cabinets. It’s physically out of sight unless you jump or look up.
*   **The Outside Chair:** Check the area where you parked the truck; sometimes a chair spawns or gets kicked outside during the "fight" that preceded your arrival.
*   **Floating Blood:** Occasionally, the game's physics can cause blood to "float" near the pool table or bowling machinery. If your sensor is pinging but the floor is clean, look up.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not complete the mission until you have retrieved the **Blackmail Files**. These are located in the back office area and are required to progress the story and understand the "deal" with Big Jim.

## True Cleaner Mode and Rewards

If you are playing on the [official Steam version](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1040200/Crime_Scene_Cleaner/) of the game, completing "Town Too Small" on **True Cleaner** mode unlocks an exclusive skin for your equipment. True Cleaner mode removes many of the HUD assists, making the search for those tiny blood splatters much more difficult.

1.  **Preparation:** Ensure your "Pat on the Back" skills are leveled up in the General category for better efficiency.
2.  **Efficiency:** Always fill your water buckets in the maintenance room sink before starting a new room to minimize travel time.
3.  **The Ending:** After cleaning, you will trigger a cutscene where the protagonist reflects on his survival and the bodies he has disposed of. This sets the stage for future updates and potential Act 3 content.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the door handle in the Town Too Small mission?**

A: The door handle is located in the room directly behind the bowling lanes. It is sitting on a desk and is required to unlock the secret room in the maintenance area.

**Q: How many bodies are in the crime scene cleaner town too small level?**

A: There are a total of 8 bodies that must be bagged and disposed of in your truck to complete the primary objectives.

**Q: I can't find the last bit of blood in the bowling alley. Where is it?**

A: Check the very back of the bowling pin reset machinery and the ceiling directly above the lanes. Blood often splatters onto the mechanical parts where it is hard to see without a flashlight.

**Q: Is "Town Too Small" the final mission of the game?**

A: As of the 2026 Act 2 update, it is one of the final challenges currently available. However, the ending cutscene strongly suggests that more missions will be added in future updates as the protagonist begins working on "his own terms."
