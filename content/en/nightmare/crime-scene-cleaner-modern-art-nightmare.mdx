Navigating the **crime scene cleaner modern art nightmare** mission is a significant step up in difficulty compared to the standard version of the gallery. In this twisted version of Kovalsky’s mind, the linear path of the museum is replaced by a surreal network of portals and floating furniture. To achieve a perfect score, you must not only scrub every drop of blood but also solve complex spatial puzzles involving misplaced exhibits and skeletal remains.

This **crime scene cleaner modern art nightmare** walkthrough covers the central "Cloud Level" hub, the specific logic behind painting placement, and the location of every piece of evidence. Whether you are hunting for the elusive Onyx Disc or struggling with the T-Rex assembly, follow these expert strategies to clean up the gallery and secure your Steam achievements in 2026.

## The Cloud Level: Portal Hub Guide

In Nightmare mode, the gallery is no longer a set of connected hallways. Instead, you operate out of the **Cloud Level**, a central hub that acts as an elevator to various "Exhibitions." Understanding which portal leads where is the first step to efficiency.

| Portal Appearance | Destination Exhibition | Key Objectives |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Black Marble** | Foyer | Entrance cleaning, painting drop-off |
| **White** | Furniture Exhibition | White & Blue/Yellow rooms, Dagger evidence |
| **Black & White** | Cafeteria | Trash disposal, various T-Rex parts |
| **Red** | Duck Room | **Onyx Disc location**, 2 Bodies |
| **Grey Marble** | Music Exhibition | Cassette/Carpet rooms, Snare drums |
| **Blue** | Upper Passage | Animal sculptures, Colorful paintings |
| **Bronze** | Sewage Exhibition | **TV Puzzle**, Body #9 |
| **Green** | Ancient Exhibition | T-Rex assembly, Statues |

> **💡 Tip:** The Cloud Level serves as your primary deposit area for bodies and garbage bags. Use the water tap here to refill your buckets frequently, as the Nightmare grime accumulates faster than usual.

## Mastering the Painting Logic

The most frustrating part of the **crime scene cleaner modern art nightmare** mission is the sheer volume of paintings (roughly 30) that must be returned to their "proper" areas. While the game doesn't require a specific spot on the wall for each individual piece, they must be returned to the correct room category.

### Painting Categories by Visual Style

1.  **Nature & Warm Colors:** These feature forests, houses, and earthy tones. They belong in the **Foyer** (the starting area).
2.  **Abstract "Child-like" Art:** If the painting looks like a chaotic splash of vibrant colors (often described as "made by a five-year-old"), it belongs in the **Upper Passage** (Colorful Paintings section).
3.  **Gold-Bordered Classics:** Any painting with a thick, ornate gold frame must be placed in the **L-shaped hallway** that connects the Upper Passage to the Music and Ancient exhibitions.

## Room-by-Room Checklist

To hit 100% completion, you must use your "Sense" to detect misplaced items. A room is not considered "correct" until every item belonging elsewhere is removed and every item belonging there is returned.

### The Furniture Exhibition (White & Blue Rooms)
This area is a blend of the former Red and Yellow rooms. You will find a person lying on a white table and a **Dagger** evidence piece in their back.
*   **Hidden Item:** Look for a specific pillow in the Blue and Yellow room that is hard to see even with screening sense; it often hides in the corners of the yellow section.
*   **Key Move:** The **Yellow Globe** found here actually belongs in the Music Exhibition (Cassette Room).

### The Music Exhibition
Split into the Cassette Room and the Carpet Room, this area requires heavy assembly of instruments.
*   **Instruments:** You must find the Snare Drum, Hi-Hat, Crash Cymbal, and Ride Cymbal. Many of these are scattered in the Ancient and Sewage exhibitions.
*   **Misplaced Item Tip:** If your scanner indicates a misplaced item in the Music Exhibition but you can't see it, check the **Secret Briefing Room** in the Upper Passage—the game often counts it as part of the Music exhibit's logic.

### The Sewage Exhibition & TV Puzzle
The Sewage Exhibition contains the only major logic puzzle in the level. To progress and find Body #9, you must interact with the six TV screens.

| Puzzle Component | Required Action | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **TV Screens** | Set all 6 screens to **Green** | Unlocks the gate to the body |
| **Basketball Hoop** | Throw the ball through the hoop | Pops out a hidden Ancient Art piece |

| Item Type | Location | Specific Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when cleaning the paintings in the Upper Passage. One specific painting has blood on it that is nearly invisible even with your cleaning sense. If you are stuck at 97% blood removal, scrub the frames of the large abstract paintings.

## The Ancient Exhibition: T-Rex Assembly

The T-Rex requires 18 different parts to be fully restored. These are intentionally scattered across almost every portal destination.

*   **Jaw:** Found on the floor of the Cloud Level.
*   **Toe:** Hidden in the Duck Exhibition (inside a pottery tower).
*   **Tail:** Located on a couch in the Cafeteria (upstairs balcony).
*   **Arm:** Held by the drummer mannequin in the Music Exhibition stage.
*   **Leg Bone:** Found at the Magic Doors near the cafeteria entrance.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I solve the TV puzzle in the crime scene cleaner modern art nightmare?**

A: You must turn all six TV screens in the Sewage Exhibition to the color green. You can find a hint for this setup in the Surveillance Room of the Music Exhibition.

**Q: Where is the missing pillow in the Modern Art gallery?**

A: The pillow is located in the Blue and Yellow room of the Furniture Exhibition. It is often the last item players find because it blends into the environment and is difficult to highlight with the scanner.

**Q: I’ve cleaned everything but I’m missing 1% of the blood. Where is it?**

A: Check the paintings in the Upper Passage. Blood can splatter onto the artwork itself, and because of the colorful nature of the "nightmare" art, the red stains are extremely difficult to see without standing directly in front of the canvas.

**Q: How do I find the Onyx Disc/Cassette in this level?**

A: Go to the Ducks Exhibition via the red portal. Head upstairs and look for an open red door. At the end of that corridor, there is a concealed door that leads to a small labyrinth. The music box containing the disc is at the end of this path.
