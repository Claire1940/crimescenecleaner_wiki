## Factors Influencing Your Cleaning Crime Scenes Salary

The base **cleaning crime scenes salary** for any given mission is set by the client, but the final number on your check is rarely that simple. The game rewards "perfectionists" who leave a scene looking like nothing ever happened. If you leave behind a single blood splatter or a stray piece of evidence, your client will dock your pay significantly. Conversely, finishing a job with 100% completion often triggers a "Professionalism Bonus."

Beyond the basic requirements, your equipment level plays a massive role. Using a basic mop on a large-scale massacre will take hours, dragging down your hourly earnings. Investing in power washers and industrial-grade detergents allows you to flip scenes faster, effectively increasing your daily income.

To truly maximize your **cleaning crime scenes salary**, you need to invest your skill points wisely. The game features a robust skill tree that mirrors real-world certifications. ## High-Value Contracts and Mission Tiers

Not all crimes are created equal. As you progress through the 2026 campaign, you will unlock different tiers of missions. While "Residential Cleanups" are good for beginners, the real money is in "Corporate "Accidents" and "Underworld Sanitize" missions. These high-tier jobs offer a much higher **cleaning crime scenes salary** but come with increased risks, such as police patrols or complex environmental hazards like electricity and gas leaks.

1. **The Apartment (Tier 1):** Low pay, but great for practicing basic mechanics and finding easy loot.
2. **The Nightclub (Tier 2):** High volume of trash and glass. Requires upgraded trash capacity.
3. **The Research Lab (Tier 3):** Requires specialized chemicals to neutralize bio-agents. Very high payout.
4. **The Senator's Estate (Tier 4):** Massive area. The **cleaning crime scenes salary** here is the highest in the game, but the 100% completion requirement is brutal.

> **Warning:** On Tier 3 and 4 missions, failing to dispose of bodies in the correct sequence can lead to a "Cleanup Failure," resulting in zero pay and a loss of reputation.

## The Ethics of "Finders Keepers"

A unique mechanic in the game that directly impacts your **cleaning crime scenes salary** is the ability to steal. While your clients pay you to clean, the victims often leave behind cash, jewelry, and high-end electronics. 

You have a choice: you can "recover" these items for a small honesty bonus from the client, or you can "pocket" them to sell on the black market. Stealing offers a much higher immediate financial reward, but it carries the risk of lowering your "Trust" rating with certain clients. In the 2026 version of the game, maintaining a balance is key. If your trust gets too low, you will lose access to the high-paying "Elite" contracts, which eventually hurts your long-term earnings.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the average cleaning crime scenes salary for a mid-game mission?**

A: By mid-game (around level 10-15), you should be earning between 3,000 and 5,500 credits per mission, provided you achieve at least a 95% cleanliness rating and find most of the hidden valuables.

**Q: Does the difficulty setting affect my cleaning crime scenes salary?**

A: Yes. Playing on "Professional" or "Nightmare" difficulty adds a multiplier to your base pay (typically 1.5x or 2.0x), but it also makes blood harder to see and increases the amount of trash you must haul away.

**Q: Can I replay missions to grind for more money?**

A: Absolutely. Replaying earlier missions with late-game gear is one of the most effective ways to farm credits. You can finish Tier 1 missions in minutes using the Industrial Power Washer, allowing you to stack your **cleaning crime scenes salary** quickly.

**Q: What happens if I get caught stealing by the police or clients?**

A: If you are caught, you will face a massive fine that is deducted from your current mission's pay. In some cases, it can result in a negative balance for the job, meaning you actually lose money for your work. Always ensure the "Detection" meter is low before pocketing expensive items.
