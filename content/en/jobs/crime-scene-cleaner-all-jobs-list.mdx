## Detailed Mission Walkthroughs

Navigating the **crime scene cleaner all jobs list** requires a specific set of skills for each environment. Below are the highlights and strategies for the most pivotal missions in the game.

### Early Game: Learning the Ropes
The first few missions, such as **Bad Call** and **Toxic Love**, serve as an introduction to the core mechanics. You will focus on basic blood scrubbing and body disposal. 

*   **Bad Call:** Focus on the bathroom and the main living area. Don't forget to check under the rug for hidden blood pools.
*   **Toxic Love:** This introduces the power washer. Use it to clear the large patio area quickly, but be careful not to break expensive vases that can be stolen for extra cash.

> **💡 Tip:** Always use your "Cleaner Vision" (Q key) frequently. It highlights missed blood spots and interactive items that are easy to overlook in low-light environments.

### Mid-Game: Increasing Complexity
By the time you reach **Italian Job** and **Affairs of the State**, the "All Jobs List" starts demanding better resource management. You will need to upgrade your bucket capacity and mop durability to handle the sheer volume of waste.

*   **Affairs of the State:** This mission is notorious for having blood on the ceilings. Ensure you have the ladder or the long-reach mop upgrade before attempting to 100% this level.
*   **Sound of Silence:** Pay close attention to the acoustic foam on the walls. Blood soaks into these surfaces, requiring deep cleaning with the sponge rather than just a quick mop swipe.

### Late Game: The Cleaning Elite
The final tier of the **crime scene cleaner all jobs list** features massive maps like **Trial by Fire** and **Dead on Arrival**. These missions can take upwards of 90 minutes to complete fully.

1.  **Trial by Fire:** You must navigate a burnt-out warehouse. The combination of soot and blood makes surfaces doubly hard to clean. Use the industrial-grade detergent here.
2.  **Dead on Arrival:** The final job. It involves multiple floors, elevator puzzles, and a staggering amount of evidence to collect. Ensure your inventory is fully upgraded to carry all the "trash" out in fewer trips.

## Secrets and Collectibles in Every Job

Every entry in the **crime scene cleaner all jobs list** contains hidden secrets. These are not required for the basic mission completion but are essential for the "Perfectionist" achievement and for lore enthusiasts.

*   **Cassette Tapes:** Each level has a hidden music track. These are often tucked away in ventilation shafts or behind movable furniture.
*   **Evidence:** Beyond the bodies, you must find incriminating items like flash drives, folders, and weapons. Failing to find all evidence results in a lower payout from your mob bosses.
*   **Stolen Goods:** Look for watches, jewelry, and stacks of cash. While the mob pays you a flat fee, the items you "liberate" from the crime scenes provide the bulk of your upgrade currency.

## Strategies for 100% Completion

If you want to check off every item on your **crime scene cleaner all jobs list** with a five-star rating, follow this workflow:

1.  **Body and Evidence First:** Always remove the "hot" items first. If the police arrive (a rare scripted event in some missions), having the bodies gone is your priority.
2.  **Large Trash Removal:** Clear out the furniture and broken items to give yourself a clear floor space for mopping.
3.  **Top-Down Cleaning:** Start with the ceilings and walls. Gravity causes water and blood to drip down; if you clean the floor first, you'll just have to do it again.
4.  **Final Sweep:** Use the Pulse Scanner to find that last 1% of blood that is inevitably hiding behind a door hinge or under a couch.

## FAQ

**Q: How many missions are on the Crime Scene Cleaner all jobs list?**

A: As of the 2026 update, there are 12 main story missions, along with 3 "side-hustle" challenge maps that focus on speed-cleaning rather than narrative.

**Q: Can I replay jobs from the list to earn more money?**

A: Yes, you can access any completed mission via the computer in your home base. This is the best way to farm cash for the high-end tool upgrades.

**Q: What happens if I leave a crime scene without cleaning 100% of the blood?**

A: You can finish a mission at any time once the "minimum requirements" are met, but your payout will be significantly reduced, and you may receive a threatening email from your employer.

**Q: Is there a secret ending on the crime scene cleaner all jobs list?**

A: Yes. To unlock the "Good Ending," you must find all the secret folders in every mission and choose specific dialogue options during the final confrontation in the **Dead on Arrival** job.

**Q: Which job is the hardest to 100%?**

A: Most players consider **Trial by Fire** to be the hardest due to the overlapping layers of soot, ash, and blood, which makes the Cleaner Vision less effective than in other levels.
