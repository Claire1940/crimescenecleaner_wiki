## Mission Overview: The Modern Art Gallery

The "Modern Art" mission takes place in a sprawling museum where a "murderous game" was hosted for wealthy VIPs. Your primary objective is to clean up after 10 participants who fought to the death. The map is divided into several major zones: the Central Exhibition, West Wing, East Wing, North Exhibition, Upper Passage, and the Cafeteria.

Because this is one of the largest maps in the game, players should focus on unlocking high-tier cleaning skills before attempting a full completionist run. Efficiency is key when you are dealing with dozens of misplaced pieces of furniture and scattered dinosaur bones.

> **💡 Tip:** Always prioritize collecting the T-Rex pieces first. They are scattered across multiple exhibits, and bringing them back to the Central Exhibition early prevents you from losing track of them during the final scrubbing phase.

## The Two Major Museum Secrets

There are two primary **crime scene cleaner museum secrets** that grant access to hidden areas, exclusive loot, and the final cassette tape. These are not merely hidden behind doors; they require active interaction with the environment.

### 1. The TV Puzzle (Northern Exhibition)
Deep within the Northern Exhibition, near the dark labyrinth, you will find a room filled with broken television sets. This is the key to the first major secret.

*   **Observation:** Before entering the maze, look at the ceiling in the preceding room. You will see a specific pattern of colors displayed on overhead screens.
*   **The Solution:** Navigate to the far left corner of the room with the broken TVs. You must interact with the screens to match the static color to the ceiling's reference.
*   **The Reward:** Solving this opens a secret door on the right. Inside, you can pick up a Katana, which triggers a combat minigame against mannequins. Completing this mini-game grants the **Rage Against the Machine** achievement and leads to the final cassette tape.

### 2. The Cassette Wall Staircase (West Wing)
Located in the West Wing's music room (near the band area), this secret requires some platforming skills.

*   **The Setup:** Look at the wall to find several large, transparent cassette tape decorations.
*   **The Process:** Interact with the lowest cassette to make it slide out from the wall. Jump onto it, then interact with the next one up. Continue this "staircase" effect until you reach the very top.
*   **The Trigger:** At the top of the climb, there is a much smaller cassette decoration. Interact with it to slide open a hidden door in the wall.
*   **The Reward:** This room contains high-value paintings and a significant amount of cash loot.

## Body Locations and Evidence

To clear the mission with a perfect score, you must locate all 10 bodies and gather all pieces of evidence. The museum's verticality can make this difficult, especially in the "Many Doors" exhibit.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I solve the TV color puzzle in the museum?**

A: You must match the colors on the six broken TVs in the Northern Exhibition to the colors shown on the ceiling screens in the previous room. The order is: Pink (UL), Orange (LL), White (UM), Blue (LM), Green (UR), and White (LR). This is one of the most vital **crime scene cleaner museum secrets** for unlocking the Katana.

**Q: Where is the missing T-Rex jaw?**

A: The T-Rex jaw is usually found on the floor in the Central Exhibition, but other bones like the leg bone are in the "Two Door Room" and the toe claw is near the "Many Doors" exhibit.

**Q: What happens if I pick up the Katana?**

A: Picking up the Katana triggers a combat event where you must destroy spawning mannequins. Successfully completing this clears the path to the final cassette tape and unlocks the "Rage Against the Machine" achievement.

**Q: I can't find the last piece of furniture in the Central Exhibition. Where is it?**

A: Check the fire extinguisher and the tilted paintings. Often, players forget that "fixing" the museum includes straightening the art on the walls and placing the fire extinguishers back into their wall-mounted lockers.
