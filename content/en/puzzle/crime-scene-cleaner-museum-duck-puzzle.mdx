## Understanding the Museum Duck Puzzle

Located in one of the side galleries of the Modern Art mission, the duck installation consists of a row of rubber ducks that emit different musical notes when interacted with. Beside the smaller ducks sits a much larger "master" duck. This has led many players to believe there is a specific melody required to unlock a hidden compartment or achievement.

However, based on high-level playtesting and community data in 2026, the **crime scene cleaner museum duck puzzle** appears to function primarily as an interactive "Easter Egg" or musical toy rather than a tracked secret. The ducks are arranged like a standard piano keyboard, starting from the note C and ending at B. The "missing" ducks in the sequence correspond to the natural lack of semitones (black keys) between E/F and B/C in music theory.

| Feature | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Location** | Side room near the "Red Room" entrance. |
| **Mechanism** | Musical notes triggered by interaction (C-D-E-F-G-A-B). |
| **Tracked Secret?** | **No**. It does not count toward the "Secrets" counter. |
| **Purpose** | Flavor text/Environment interaction. |

> **💡 Tip:** While the ducks are fun to play with, don't spend hours trying to find a "solution" melody. If you are looking to 100% the mission, focus your energy on the TV Puzzle and the Cassette Jumping Puzzle instead.

## How to Solve the TV Maze Puzzle (Secret #2)

If you are looking for the actual "puzzle" that grants a secret credit, you need to head to the maze-like installation featuring various television screens. This is often where players get stuck, as the solution requires a keen eye for environmental clues.

Follow these steps to unlock the secret door:

1.  **Identify the Clue:** Before entering the maze, look at the ceiling of the room. You will see a specific pattern of colors displayed on overhead monitors.
2.  **Navigate the Maze:** Enter the maze and head to the far left corner where a cluster of broken, static-filled TVs are located.
3.  **Match the Colors:** Interact with the TVs to change their static color. You must match them exactly to the sequence shown on the ceiling monitors.
4.  **Enter the Secret Room:** Once the colors are correct, a hidden door will slide open to your right.

Inside this room, you will find a Katana. Picking up this weapon triggers a unique combat event against mannequins. Successfully defending yourself will open the exit and allow a new mannequin to spawn outside holding a cassette tape. This also unlocks the **"Rage Against the Machine"** achievement.

## All Cassette Tape Locations in Modern Art

There are four cassette tapes hidden throughout the museum. Collecting these is essential for players who want to enjoy the full [Crime Scene Cleaner soundtrack](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1040200/Crime_Scene_Cleaner/) while they work.

| Tape # | Location | How to Obtain |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | Red Room Entrance | Found on a table near the fire alarm immediately inside the red door. |
| **2** | Concert Stage | Fix the mannequins on the stage. The lights will dim, music plays, and the tape appears on the floor. |
| **3** | Upstairs Corridor | Located near the duck room. Jump the barrier and go up the stairs; it's on the floor near a crate. |
| **4** | Katana Room | Spawns on a mannequin outside the secret TV room after you complete the Katana event. |

## The Cassette Wall Jumping Secret (Secret #1)

The first official secret of the mission is located in the same room as the concert stage (where you found Cassette Tape #2). This is a platforming challenge that requires precision.

1.  **Locate the Wall:** Look for a wall decorated with large, transparent cassette tape models.
2.  **Activate the Platforms:** These decorations are interactive. Clicking them causes them to slide out from the wall, creating a temporary platform.
3.  **The Climb:** Start with the lowest cassette. Click it, jump on top, then click the next one higher up. You must be quick, as they eventually slide back in.
4.  **The Hidden Switch:** At the very top of the wall, there is a miniature cassette decoration. Interacting with this small switch opens a hidden door high on the wall.
5.  **The Reward:** Jump into the opening to find a hidden stash of money and rare paintings. This counts as the first official secret for the mission.

## Essential Cleaning Tips for Modern Art

Because this map is so large, efficiency is key. You don't want to spend 4 hours cleaning only to realize you missed a bloodstain behind a statue.

*   **Use the Mop Upgrade:** High-capacity mops are essential here due to the sheer volume of blood in the "Red Room."
*   **Body Disposal:** Ensure all bodies are bagged and moved to the extraction point before you start the deep clean, as moving them later can create new messes.
*   **Check the Ceilings:** The Modern Art mission features high ceilings and hanging installations that often catch blood splatter from explosions.
*   **Inventory Management:** Keep your bucket close. The walk back to water sources in this museum is significantly longer than in previous missions.

> **Warning:** Be careful when using the power washer near the fragile art installations. Damaging the museum's property can result in heavy financial penalties at the end of the mission.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the crime scene cleaner museum duck puzzle give an achievement?**

A: No, playing the ducks does not grant a specific achievement or count toward the secrets total. The main achievements in this level are related to finding the Katana and completing the cleanup with a high rating.

**Q: I matched the TV colors but the door didn't open. What's wrong?**

A: Double-check the ceiling clue. Sometimes the perspective from the ground makes it easy to swap two colors. Also, ensure you have interacted with every TV in the cluster to "lock" the sequence.

**Q: Where is the Katana located?**

A: The Katana is inside the secret room unlocked by the TV color puzzle. Picking it up is required to trigger the mannequin fight and obtain the fourth cassette tape.

**Q: How many secrets are officially tracked in the Modern Art mission?**

A: There are two officially tracked secrets: the Cassette Wall jumping puzzle and the TV Maze color puzzle. While the **crime scene cleaner museum duck puzzle** is a popular point of interest, it is not one of the two tracked secrets.
