## Modern Art Mission Overview

The Modern Art level is widely considered the penultimate challenge in *Crime Scene Cleaner*. Set within a sprawling, multi-story gallery, the mission involves cleaning up a massacre amidst priceless (and often bizarre) exhibits. Unlike earlier missions, Modern Art relies heavily on verticality and hidden pathways.

Before diving into the puzzles, ensure you have upgraded your cleaning skills, particularly your movement speed and bucket capacity, as the sheer size of the museum can lead to significant backtracking.

## How to Solve the Crime Scene Cleaner Museum TV Puzzle

The **crime scene cleaner museum tv puzzle** is located in the television exhibit wing. To find it, start from the main hall featuring the massive T-Rex skeleton. From the T-Rex, head left and pass through the green door on your right. This leads into a maze-like section filled with monitors.

### Step 1: Finding the Color Clue
The solution to the puzzle isn't randomized, but it is hidden in plain sight within the environment. There are two ways to identify the required colors:
1.  **The Ceiling Reflection:** In the room with the broken TVs, look directly up at the ceiling. In certain lighting conditions, the correct color sequence is reflected or displayed on overhead monitors.
2.  **The Rug Exhibit Backroom:** If the ceiling is too dark to read, head to the rug exhibit. In the backroom (the one with the "don't come to work" note), there is a functional set of monitors displaying the exact color order needed for the puzzle.

### Step 2: Inputting the Sequence
Once you have the colors, return to the far left corner of the TV exhibit. You will see a cluster of broken, static-filled monitors. You must interact with these screens to cycle through their colors until they match the clue.

| Monitor Position | Required Action | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Left Screen** | Cycle to match clue color | Static changes hue |
| **Center Screen** | Cycle to match clue color | Static changes hue |
| **Right Screen** | Cycle to match clue color | **Secret Door Opens** |

> **⚠️ Important Note:** If the TV screens appear solid grey and you cannot see any colors or static, you likely have a graphics setting conflict. Ensure that **Ambient Occlusion** is turned on in your video settings. Without this enabled, the puzzle colors may not render correctly in the 2026 build of the game.

## Unlocking the Katana and Secret #2

Successfully completing the **crime scene cleaner museum tv puzzle** triggers a secret door to slide open to the right of the monitors. Inside, you will find a dimly lit room that looks starkly different from the rest of the museum.

At the end of this room sits a Katana. Picking it up will trigger a combat event. Mannequins will spawn and attack; use the Katana to defend yourself. Once all enemies are "cleaned up," the exit will open, and a new mannequin will appear outside holding **Cassette Tape #4**. This sequence also grants the "Rage Against the Machine" achievement.

## Locating All Cassette Tapes in Modern Art

While the TV puzzle is the most complex, the other collectibles in this mission require a keen eye for detail and some platforming skills.

### Cassette Tape #1: The Red Room
This is the easiest tape to find. At the very start of the mission, look for a red door on your right. Enter the room and look at the table near the fire alarm. The tape is sitting right there.

### Cassette Tape #2: The Concert Stage
This tape is tied to a small environmental interaction.
1.  Enter the room with the concert stage behind the glass.
2.  Go through the side door to get behind the display.
3.  Fix the displaced mannequins on the stage and remove any bodies.
4.  Once the "set" is restored, the lights will flicker and music will play. The tape will appear on the stage floor.

### Cassette Tape #3: The Duck Room Upstairs
From the starting area, head toward the room filled with duck sculptures. To the right, there is a staircase blocked by a barrier. Jump over the barrier and head to the upper floor. Follow the corridor to the right; the tape is on the floor next to some storage boxes.

## Finding Secret #1: The Cassette Wall

The first secret of the mission (separate from the TV puzzle) is located in the same area as the concert stage. On one of the walls, you will notice large, transparent cassette decorations. These aren't just for show—they are interactive platforms.

| Step | Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1** | Interact with the lowest wall cassette to extend it. |
| **2** | Jump onto the extended cassette. |
| **3** | Continue interacting with the cassettes above you to create a "staircase." |
| **4** | At the very top, look for a small, hidden cassette switch. |
| **5** | Activate it to open a wall panel leading to a room full of cash and rare paintings. |

## Technical Troubleshooting for 2026

As *Crime Scene Cleaner* has received various updates, some players on newer hardware might encounter issues with the museum's lighting and puzzles. 

> **💡 Tip:** If you find the museum too dark to navigate, use the "Sense" ability frequently. It highlights interactable objects, including the TV screens for the puzzle, even if the textures aren't loading properly.

| Issue | Solution |
| :--- | :--- |
| **TVs are solid grey** | Enable **Ambient Occlusion** in Graphics Settings. |
| **Katana doesn't spawn** | Ensure all blood is cleaned in the immediate vicinity of the puzzle. |
| **Mannequins won't die** | Use the heavy attack (Right Click) to break their animations. |
| **Cassette #4 missing** | You must kill all mannequins in the secret room for it to appear outside. |

You can find more community-sourced fixes on the [Official Steam Discussion Page](https://steamcommunity.com/app/1040200/discussions/) for the game.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I solve the crime scene cleaner museum tv puzzle before cleaning the room?**

A: Yes, you can solve the puzzle as soon as you enter the room. However, it is generally safer to clear the floor of glass and blood first so you don't slip or track footprints while trying to match the colors.

**Q: What happens if I lose the Katana?**

A: The Katana is a temporary "event" weapon used for the secret room's combat encounter. While you cannot keep it as a permanent tool for your cleaning arsenal, picking it up permanently unlocks the associated achievement for your profile.

**Q: I found the colors but the door won't open. What am I doing wrong?**

A: Double-check the order. The sequence must be read from left to right as displayed in the clue (either on the ceiling or in the rug exhibit backroom). If it still fails, try cycling each TV through a full loop of colors once to "reset" the interaction.

**Q: Is the Modern Art mission the final level?**

A: No, Modern Art is the penultimate (second to last) mission. Completing it with 100% secrets and cassettes will set you up perfectly for the final cleanup challenge of the game.
