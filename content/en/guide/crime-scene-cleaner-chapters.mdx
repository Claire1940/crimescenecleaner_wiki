## Comprehensive List of Crime Scene Cleaner Chapters

The game is divided into distinct Acts, with Act 1 serving as the foundation of the story and Act 2 expanding the narrative into even darker territory. Each chapter represents a specific "job" assigned by the mob or other shady characters. Completing these missions with a 100% deception score is the ultimate goal for any completionist.

Act 1 introduces you to the core mechanics of the game. You start with basic tools—a mop, a bucket, and a sponge—and gradually work your way up to industrial-grade equipment. The early **crime scene cleaner chapters** focus on teaching you how to manage waste, dispose of bodies, and clean bloodstains efficiently.

## Mastering Nightmare Mode

For those who find the standard difficulty too easy, every mission in the **crime scene cleaner chapters** can be played in Nightmare Mode. This mode increases the amount of grime, adds more hidden evidence, and often places items in more difficult-to-reach locations.

Completing Nightmare missions is required for several high-tier achievements, such as "Sweet Dreams," which is awarded for finishing all Nightmare missions.

*   **Bad Call (Nightmare):** Rewards the "Pictures Of You" achievement.
*   **Trial by Blood (Nightmare):** Rewards the "Welcome To The Jungle" achievement.
*   **Short Circuit (Nightmare):** Rewards the "Rocket Man" achievement.
*   **Modern Art (Nightmare):** Rewards the "Bad to the Bone" achievement.

> **⚠️ Warning:** In Nightmare Mode, tipping over your bucket is much more punishing. Use the "Steady Hand" skill to minimize accidents that create new stains.

## Secrets and Collectibles

Every chapter contains hidden items that flesh out the lore or provide financial bonuses. Finding these is often tied to specific achievements.

1.  **Music Cassettes:** There are dozens of cassettes hidden across the **crime scene cleaner chapters**. Finding them all unlocks the "Juke Box Hero" achievement.
2.  **Hidden Safes:** Locations like the "Trial by Blood" basement and the "Short Circuit" industrial site contain safes that require codes found in nearby notes. Opening these provides a significant cash boost.
3.  **The V.I.P Card:** Found in the "Short Circuit" mission, this card is essential for accessing restricted areas in later missions.
4.  **Zack’s Secret Room:** A well-hidden area in the "Modern Art Museum" that contains unique lore items.

## True Cleaner Mode

True Cleaner Mode is a separate challenge tier that removes many of the HUD helpers, such as the mini-map and the "Cleaner Sense" highlight. This mode is intended for veteran players who have memorized the layouts of the **crime scene cleaner chapters**. 

The "Alter Ego" and "Development Hell" missions are specifically tuned for this mode, offering a "behind the scenes" look at the game's development while providing some of the most difficult cleanup puzzles in the 2026 update.

## FAQ

**Q: How many crime scene cleaner chapters are there in total?**

A: As of the 2026 update, there are 10 main chapters in Act 1 (including the prologue) and 4 major chapters in Act 2, plus several "True Cleaner" and "Nightmare" variations.

**Q: Can I replay missions to get a perfect score?**

A: Yes, you can access any of the **crime scene cleaner chapters** from the computer in your home base. Replaying missions is the best way to earn extra cash for upgrades and find missed collectibles.

**Q: What happens if I break items during a cleanup?**

A: Breaking items like vases or electronics will lower your "Deception Score." To get the "Smooth Operator" achievement, you must complete a mission without breaking anything or creating new stains.

**Q: Is there a way to carry more trash at once?**

A: Yes, you should focus on the "General" skill tree. Upgrading your strength allows you to carry more bags, and unlocking the industrial bin tool helps you manage large amounts of waste in chapters like "Short Circuit" and "Party's Over."
